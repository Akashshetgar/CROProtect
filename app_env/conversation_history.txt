1. **Plant resistant tomato varieties**: Choose tomato varieties that are resistant to Late Blight if possible.

2. **Proper plant spacing**: Provide adequate space between tomato plants to improve air circulation and reduce humidity, which can contribute to the spread of the disease.

3. **Avoid overhead watering**: Water the plants at the base to avoid wetting the foliage, which can help prevent the spread of the disease.

4. **Rotate crops**: Do not plant tomatoes in the same location every year. Rotate your crops to prevent the buildup of pathogens in the soil.

5. **Remove infected plant parts**: If you notice any signs of Late Blight on your tomato plants, remove and destroy the infected plant parts immediately to prevent the disease from spreading.

6. **Apply fungicides**: Use copper-based fungicides or other fungicides labeled for Late Blight to protect your tomato plants. Follow the instructions carefully when applying fungicides.

7. **Monitor your plants**: Regularly inspect your tomato plants for any signs of disease. Early detection can help prevent the spread of Late Blight.

Following these prevention tips can help reduce the risk of Late Blight Disease in your tomato plants.
